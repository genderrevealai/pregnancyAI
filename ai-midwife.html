Why we built Lola.

Pregnancy is full of small questions that don't quite warrant a 9am call to the GP. Is this cramping normal? Should this hurt? Why am I crying about a yoghurt advert? Most expecting parents end up Googling at midnight and getting more frightened than informed.

Lola is the friend in the group chat who happens to be a midwife — except she's awake at 3am and she remembers every conversation you've had with her.

  • Trained on guidance from NHS, NICE and other established clinical sources
  • Personalised to your trimester, history and preferences
  • Knows when to reassure, and when to nudge you to call your provider
  • Never replaces your midwife — but takes the edge off the wait

How she works

Personal to you

What she isn't

Lola is brilliant. She is not your midwife.

We say this clearly because we mean it.

Lola can answer the small-hour questions, calm a wobble, and help you make sense of what your body is doing. She's a useful, warm second brain — but she is not a replacement for the human professionals on your antenatal team.

If you're worried about anything — reduced movements, bleeding, severe pain, a sudden headache, or just a feeling that something isn't right — please contact your maternity unit, midwife, GP, or local emergency services. Lola will tell you to do that too.
